# Portfolio Project Discovery & Personal Branding Strategy Prompt

> **Purpose:** Analyze an existing software engineer portfolio codebase, then generate an AGENTS.md that encodes your unique value proposition, technical POV, and non-negotiable rules for evolving this portfolio into a personal branding tool. Use this to land jobs AND sell custom web services in your niche.

> **Output:** A single `AGENTS.md` file that any AI agent (Claude, Cursor, Antigravity, etc.) can read to understand your portfolio's direction, build systems, and hiring/sales narrative.

---

## Role

You are a senior portfolio architect hired to:
1. **Audit** an existing portfolio project (static site, full-stack app, showcase, case studies).
2. **Identify** the engineer's unique technical identity: what problems do they solve, what stack do they champion, what values do they encode?
3. **Map** the portfolio's current reach: what signals does it send to recruiters vs. potential clients for services?
4. **Generate** a bespoke `AGENTS.md` that acts as both a development playbook AND a positioning statement.

## Phase 1 — Portfolio Code Audit

**Do not execute any code.** Read-only analysis.

### 1.1 — Current State Snapshot
- What is this portfolio project? (Static site? Full-stack app? GitHub profile with README? Blog?)
- What tech stack is it built with? (Next.js, React, vanilla JS? Node/Python backend? CMS?)
- What's deployed where? (Vercel, GitHub Pages, self-hosted VPS?)
- How dated does it look/feel? Any stale content, broken links, outdated library versions?

### 1.2 — Content Audit
- **Case studies:** How many? Do they show problem → solution → impact? Or just "I built X"?
- **Projects displayed:** Which are highlighted? Which are buried? Do the most impressive ones get visual/narrative weight?
- **Contact/CTA:** Is there a clear "hire me" or "let's talk services" call-to-action? How many friction points?
- **Social proof:** Testimonials, GitHub stars, LinkedIn recommendations, blog following, open-source contributions?

### 1.3 — Technical Signals
- Does the code itself tell a story? (e.g., clean architecture, testing, deployment automation, accessibility, performance)
- Are you showing breadth or depth? (Full-stack, specialist, generalist?)
- Does the portfolio repo have a `README` that explains the vision? Does it encode *how* you think?

### 1.4 — Audience Gaps
- **For recruiters:** Can they quickly see: (a) what you've built, (b) the stack, (c) why it matters?
- **For clients:** Can they imagine hiring you? Do they see a case study matching *their* problem?
- **For peers/community:** Does it show you stay current, contribute, mentor?

## Phase 2 — Brand Identity Discovery

Answer these questions about yourself (not your resume, your *identity*):

### 2.1 — Your Technical Philosophy
- What do you believe *good software* looks like?
- What stack/approach do you champion? Why?
- What do you refuse to compromise on? (e.g., "I will not ship without tests", "accessibility is non-negotiable")
- What problem spaces energize you? (e.g., "I love systems that handle scale", "I'm obsessed with user experience", "I build tools to automate drudgery")

### 2.2 — Your Niche
- Who do you serve best? (e.g., "Indonesian SMEs scaling online", "AI startups needing solid infrastructure", "legacy systems modernization")
- What's your unfair advantage? (e.g., deep domain knowledge in IoT, 5+ years shipping at scale, understand both hardware + software, cultural/localization expertise)
- What would you *not* build for money? (Sets boundaries for client work.)

### 2.3 — Your Storytelling Angle
- What story do you want recruiters to tell about you in a meeting?
- What story would make a potential client say "yes, THIS person gets our problem"?
- Is there a through-line in your projects? (e.g., all solve "efficiency for teams", all are "human-centered tech", all bridge "local + global")

## Phase 3 — Market Positioning

### 3.1 — Job Market Positioning
- **Target title:** What's the single best job description that describes what you want to do next?
- **Salary band:** Rough range (for filtering, not stating).
- **Deal-breakers:** Remote-required? Equity? Specific tech stack? Team size?
- **Green flags:** What signals in a JD make you excited?

### 3.2 — Service/Client Positioning
- **Service offering:** 1–3 sentences. "I build [X] using [Y] for [Z]."
  - *Example:* "I build full-stack e-commerce platforms with Next.js + FastAPI for Indonesian SMEs scaling from 0 to 7-figure revenue."
- **Ideal client profile:** Company stage (pre-seed, Series A, established SME), problem they have (slow ordering system, legacy tech debt, need AI integration), budget (ballpark).
- **Pricing model:** Project-based, retainer, success-based?
- **Non-negotiables in client work:** What type of project/client would drain you? (e.g., no spec-less projects, no 2-week deadlines for complex systems, no purely cosmetic work)

## Phase 4 — Portfolio Enhancement Strategy

For each of these areas, decide: **Keep, Evolve, Build, or Remove?**

### 4.1 — Project Selection
- Pick 2–3 projects that best signal your value to your *target* job + your *target* client.
- For each: write a concise case study (problem, your approach, outcome, what you'd do differently).
- Retire or archive projects that confuse the narrative.

### 4.2 — Visual & Narrative Refresh
- Does the design reflect your brand? (Is it modern, professional, a bit bold? Or dated and safe?)
- Does every project link to a **case study**, not just a GitHub repo?
- Does the homepage have a single, crystal-clear value prop? (Not 5 bullet points — one sentence.)

### 4.3 — Social Proof Layer
- Do you have testimonials from past colleagues/clients? Gather 2–3.
- Open-source contributions? Highlight them.
- Speaking, blogging, community presence? Link to it.
- GitHub stats? (Activity, contributions, open-source projects with stars.)

### 4.4 — Service/Offering Visibility
- Is there a clear "Services" or "Work With Me" section?
- Do you have a one-pager, rate card, or service menu?
- Can a potential client easily book a call, email you, or get a proposal template?

### 4.5 — SEO & Discoverability
- Portfolio URL: is it your name, a custom domain, or buried on GitHub?
- Meta tags, Open Graph: do links preview nicely?
- Blog/content: do you have at least 3–5 technical posts that rank for relevant keywords? (e.g., "fullstack developer Indonesia", "next.js fastapi integration", "iot dashboard with mqtt")
- Backlinks: are you mentioned by clients, employers, technical publications?

## Phase 5 — Generate AGENTS.md

**Output a single `AGENTS.md` file** that encodes:

1. **Your Identity Block**
   - Your name, location, title.
   - 1–2 sentence positioning: "I am a [title] who [value prop]. I solve [problem] with [approach]."
   - Links: portfolio URL, LinkedIn, GitHub, email, calendar link.

2. **Technical Philosophy & Non-Negotiables**
   - Your tech stack stance. (What you champion, what you avoid, and why.)
   - Non-negotiable rules for code/systems you build. (Testing, accessibility, performance, security, localization.)
   - Examples: "All monetary values as integers in IDR, never floating-point." "No shipping without mobile-responsive design." "Accessibility audit is step 1, not step 10."

3. **Niche & Ideal Scope**
   - Your niche: who do you serve, what problems do you solve?
   - Geographic/cultural context if relevant. (e.g., "I build for Indonesian businesses scaling online; I understand PPN, multi-outlet, local payment gatways, regulations.")
   - Project size you thrive in: solo, 2–3 person team, larger?

4. **Job Market Positioning**
   - Target job titles & companies.
   - What excites you in a role.
   - What you're looking for: remote, equity, learning opportunity, team size, tech debt, greenfield?

5. **Service Offerings**
   - 2–3 service lines you'll sell. (e.g., "Custom e-commerce platforms", "IoT dashboard development", "AI agent tooling".)
   - For each: problem it solves, ideal client, rough timeline, example project.

6. **Portfolio Showcase Rules**
   - Which projects appear first? (Rank them by hiring signal vs. service signal.)
   - Case study format: problem → approach → outcome → what you'd do next.
   - What stays private/behind NDA; what's public.

7. **Content & Community Strategy**
   - Blog topics you'll cover. (Tie to your niche & SEO.)
   - Open-source: what you'll maintain, contribute to, or build.
   - Speaking/events: conferences, webinars, mentor roles.

8. **Client Work Filters**
   - Green flags: project size, problem type, team dynamics, learning opportunity.
   - Red flags: no spec, impossible timelines, scope creep without boundaries, only cosmetic work.
   - Pricing model: how you charge.
   - Example: "I take custom web projects ($15k–$50k), retainer relationships (minimum 3 months), or equity partnerships with early-stage startups if vision aligns."

9. **Hiring Narrative**
   - The story a recruiter should tell about you. (2–3 paragraphs that highlight your unique edge.)
   - Key projects that prove it.
   - Outcome you're optimizing for in next role.

10. **Success Metrics**
    - How do you measure portfolio success?
    - Hiring: inbound recruiter interest, job title uplevel, salary range, company type.
    - Services: inbound leads, project scope, client LTV, referral rate.
    - Community: GitHub stars, blog traffic, conference speaking, mentorship impact.

| 9+ | Iterate based on recruiter feedback | Close first paid project; document as case study | Speak at 1 local meetup or webinar |

## Do NOT

- Oversell yourself. (If you've only built projects, don't claim you're an "architect" leading teams.)
- Chase every trend. (Your tech stance should be informed, not just fashionable.)
- Hide constraints. (If you're early-career, location-locked, or new to a stack, say it clearly — smart employers respect honesty.)
- Build a portfolio that's all form, no substance. (A slow, unresponsive site signals you don't care about fundamentals.)
- Forget the reader. (Recruit or client, they're busy. Make scanning fast and context clear.)

---

## Definition of Done

Your AGENTS.md is ready when you can answer each of these:

1. **Can a recruiter in 30 seconds understand:** what you've built, what you're looking for, why you're the fit for their role?
2. **Can a potential client in 1 minute understand:** what problem you solve, how you solve it, how much it costs, and how to hire you?
3. **Does your portfolio code itself tell a story?** (Not "I know React", but "I build fast, accessible, production-grade systems".)
4. **Is there a through-line?** Your projects, content, open-source, and job targets should all point to the same vision.
5. **Are you comfortable with this being your public face for the next 12 months?** If not, it's not done.
